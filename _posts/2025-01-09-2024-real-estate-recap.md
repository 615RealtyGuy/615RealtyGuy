*2024 NAR Report Highlights: Key Insights for Prospective Buyers and Sellers*

The National Association of REALTORS® (NAR) recently released its 2024 "Profile of Home Buyers and Sellers," providing an in-depth look at the evolving housing market. This year's report highlights various buyer and seller demographics, preferences, and trends, revealing essential information for anyone planning to enter the real estate market in the coming year. Whether you're a first-time buyer, a repeat buyer, or a seller, these insights can help you navigate the current landscape.

## 1. Aging Demographics

![2024 real estate market overview](/assets/images/blog/2024-recap-market-overview.png) and Rising Buyer Ages

The median age of all home buyers has reached an all-time high of 56, and first-time buyers are now typically 38 years old. This shift is partly due to economic pressures and increased home prices, which have led many first-time buyers to wait longer before entering the market. Repeat buyers have also aged, with a median age of 61. These trends reflect the challenges young adults face in affording homes and indicate a growing focus among older adults on buying homes closer to family and friends.

## 2. Historic Low for First-Time

![First-time homebuyer statistics](/assets/images/blog/2024-recap-first-time-buyers.png) Home Buyers

First-time home buyers represent only 24% of the market, a historic low. Rising home prices and higher mortgage interest rates, which averaged 7.02% over the year, have made it harder for newcomers. For first-time buyers, the median household income needed is now $97,000, underscoring the financial commitment required to buy a home today. Repeat buyers benefit from accumulated equity and larger down payments, giving them an edge in this competitive environment.

## 3. Multi-Generational Housing on the Rise

Multi-generational homes now make up 17% of purchases, the highest in NAR's history. Families are increasingly pooling resources to afford housing, with common reasons including elder care and financial savings. If you're considering a home purchase, this trend might encourage you to think about family-oriented options and flexible living spaces.

## 4. Digital Home Searches and the Role of Real Estate Agents

Online tools have become integral, with 43% of buyers beginning their search online and 86% relying on real estate agents as a key information source. Mobile and tablet use is high, and online features like photos, floor plans, and property details are highly valued. Even though virtual home tours remain popular, 88% of buyers ultimately choose to work with an agent, highlighting the importance of professional guidance.

## 5. Diverse Buyer Pool

![Buyer diversity and demographics](/assets/images/blog/2024-recap-diversity.png) with Growing Representation

While 83% of buyers identify as White, 36% of first-time buyers are from non-White backgrounds, reflecting increased diversity among new homeowners. Additionally, 9% of all buyers were born outside the U.S., with an even higher share among first-time buyers (13%). This data suggests that the market is slowly becoming more inclusive, but also signals the need for diverse communities and supportive services.

## 6. Location Preferences and Neighborhood Priorities

Suburban and subdivision areas remain popular, housing 45% of recent buyers. Quality of neighborhood, proximity to family, and overall affordability are top considerations. Interestingly, job convenience, though important, has declined as a priority, indicating that factors like work-from-home flexibility may be reshaping buying preferences.

## 7. Financing and Down Payments

With only 26% of buyers making all-cash purchases — an all-time high — down payments are more significant, especially for first-time buyers. The median down payment for repeat buyers stands at 23%, while first-time buyers typically put down 9%, the highest share since 1997. Higher down payment requirements highlight the importance of financial planning and preparedness when entering the market.

## 8. Satisfaction with the Home-Buying Process

Despite the challenges, 92% of buyers report satisfaction with the buying process, thanks in part to the support of real estate agents, who assist with finding properties, negotiating, and understanding paperwork. For anyone entering the market, this statistic underscores the value of working with a skilled agent to navigate the complex buying journey.

## 9. Selling Trends

![Seller satisfaction and agent utilization](/assets/images/blog/2024-recap-seller-trends.png): High Agent Use and Fast Turnaround

In 2024, 90% of sellers used a real estate agent, and homes sold within a median of three weeks. With a focus on pricing competitively and selling within specific timeframes, agents continue to play a crucial role for sellers looking to achieve quick and satisfactory sales. FSBO (For Sale By Owner) sales are at a record low, demonstrating the value sellers place on professional representation.

## Looking Ahead to 2025

The NAR's 2024 report shows a market defined by rising age demographics, increased diversity, and growing demand for professional assistance. Whether you're buying or selling, these trends highlight the importance of preparation, financial stability, and the support of an experienced agent.
