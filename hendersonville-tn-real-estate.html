Why Hendersonville?

One of Middle Tennessee's most sought-after communities — and for good reason.

Lakefront Living

Hendersonville sits on the shores of Old Hickory Lake, offering waterfront homes, marinas, and scenic views just 20 minutes from downtown Nashville.

Top-Rated Schools

Sumner County schools consistently rank among the best in the state, making Hendersonville a top pick for families with children.

Easy Nashville Commute

With direct access via Vietnam Veterans Blvd and Gallatin Pike, most Hendersonville residents reach downtown Nashville in 25 minutes or less.

Music City Heritage

Home to the legendary Johnny Cash and June Carter Cash, Hendersonville has deep roots in Tennessee's music and cultural history.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about buying and selling in Hendersonville, TN.

What is the average home price in Hendersonville, TN?

As of 2026, the median home price in Hendersonville is in the mid-$400K range, though prices vary significantly by neighborhood. Lakefront and newer construction homes command premium prices, while established neighborhoods offer more affordable options. I provide clients with detailed, up-to-date CMAs so you know exactly what to expect.

Is Hendersonville a good place to live?

Absolutely. Hendersonville consistently ranks among the best places to live in Tennessee thanks to excellent schools, low crime rates, lakefront recreation, and an easy commute to Nashville. The city has a strong sense of community with local events, parks, and dining options that make it ideal for families and professionals alike.

How do I find the best realtor in Hendersonville, TN?

Look for an agent with real local market knowledge — someone who tracks neighborhood-level data, understands pricing trends, and has experience closing deals in Hendersonville specifically. I live and work in this area, use data-driven analytics to advise my clients, and focus on building long-term relationships rather than chasing volume.

What neighborhoods in Hendersonville are best for families?

Durham Farms, Drakes Creek, and Mansker Farms are popular choices for families thanks to newer construction, community amenities, and proximity to top-rated Sumner County schools. Indian Lake and Saundersville Road areas are great for those wanting more space and lake access. I help families match their priorities — schools, commute, budget — to the right neighborhood.

How long does it take to sell a home in Hendersonville?

Market conditions fluctuate, but well-priced homes in Hendersonville typically sell within 15–45 days. Factors like pricing strategy, home condition, and seasonal timing all play a role. I help sellers prepare a strategy that minimizes days on market while maximizing sale price.
